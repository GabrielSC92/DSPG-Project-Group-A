## Part 1: Understanding the Problem

### Context
*"Het ontbreken van integrerende inzichten in kwaliteit van overheid en beleid is een probleem. Het is allereerst maatschappelijk onverstandig."*

*(The lack of integrating insights into the quality of government and policy is a problem. It is first and foremost socially unwise.)*

### Q1: Is the biggest problem a lack of centralization or insufficient data collection?

**Key Finding:** The core issue is **not** a lack of data collection, but rather the **absence of aggregation and curation**.

- Substantial high-quality data is already being collected by various institutions
- This data remains buried in reports and is not aggregated
- The priority is to curate existing data to improve accessibility and quality

### Q2: Why has there never been a central point of data collection?

**Key Finding:** The absence stems from **political and institutional factors**.

- The Central Statistics Bureau (CBS) collects certain types of data, often mirrored to other sources
- CBS's mandate has not included measuring government quality
- Political reasons contribute to this gap (e.g., limited interest from current/past governments)

### Q3: What societal issues does the database seek to tackle?

**Key Finding:** The project addresses **outcome measurement** beyond process metrics.

The database aims to provide key data in critical societal areas:
- Provision of care
- Environmental protection
- Other public services

**Current Process Measures:**
| Dimension | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Rule of Law | Legal framework adherence |
| Efficiency | Resource utilization |
| Effectiveness | Goal achievement |
| Expertise & Capacity | Institutional competence |

**Important Insight:** The project seeks to move beyond process measures to include **output dimensions** — for example, hospital performance shouldn't only be measured by overnight patients or successful surgeries, as other factors influence these numbers.

### Q4: Which parties currently collect data, and why is collaboration insufficient?

**Data Collecting Parties:**
- Plan Bureau (Planbureau)
- Supervision organizations
- CBS (Central Bureau of Statistics)
- Various other governmental bodies

**Collaboration Barrier:** The lack of collaboration is primarily due to a **lack of incentive** among these parties to share and integrate their data.

**Resource:** [Kennis van de Overheid](https://home.kennisvandeoverheid.nl/)

## Part 2: Scoping Our Project

### Framework Reference
The supervisor referenced the following quality dimensions from the vision document:

1. **Capacity** (Het gaat om de capaciteit)
2. **Competence** to deploy instruments (competentie om instrumenten in te zetten)
3. **Effectiveness** in achieving goals (doelen effectief worden gerealiseerd)
4. **Efficiency** (op een efficiënte wijze)
5. **Democratic Framework** (binnen de kaders van democratie)
6. **Rule of Law** (en rechtsstaat)

### Q1: Which metric is most important for quality measurement?

**Key Finding:** Quality is a **holistic combination** of all six dimensions.

- Quality itself is an inherently vague concept
- No single metric can capture government quality
- All dimensions must be considered together

### Q2: Which metrics are already being monitored/quantified?

**Key Finding:** All dimensions are monitored **to some extent**, but with varying quality.

- The DemocratieMonitor tracks indicators related to Rule of Law
- Example metrics include: number of protests against the government
- **Challenge:** The quality of measurement and quantification varies significantly

**Resource:** [Democratie Monitor Nederland](https://www.democratiemonitor.nl/)

### Q3: What part of government has highest monitoring priority?

**Key Finding:** **Central government** is the primary focus, with scalability options.

- Initial scope: Dutch central government
- Potential for scaling to other governmental levels
- Not intended for immediate international comparability

### Q4: What is the ideal output of this project?

**Key Finding:** A **widely accessible digital information tool**.

| Target Audience | Purpose |
|-----------------|---------|
| Regular citizens | Easy access to government performance data |
| Policymakers | Evidence-based decision support |
| Public debate | Informing civic discourse |

**Unique Value Proposition:** Counter the negativity in news media by presenting a more balanced view of government performance that may not align with media narratives.

### Q5: What freedom do we have in creating the prototype?

**Data Sources:** Everything publicly available is fair game

**Tools:** To be determined (Streamlit suggested as a possibility)

### Q6: What LLM resources and budget are available?

| Aspect | Status |
|--------|--------|
| LLM Choice | To be determined |
| Budget | External organizations, independent funding |
| Project Timeline | Current budget covers 4 years |

## Part 3: The Organization (DKO)

### International Context
*"zoals bijvoorbeeld België (Instituut voor de Overheid; Leuven) en Zweden (Quality of Government Institute; Gothenburg) dat wel kennen."*

The project draws inspiration from established international examples:
- **Belgium:** Instituut voor de Overheid (Leuven)
- **Sweden:** Quality of Government Institute (Gothenburg)

### Q1: What work has already been done?

**Current State:** Conceptual work is in progress, but no concrete products exist.

Work completed:
- Conceptual models and frameworks
- Assessment of existing data and its quality
- Evaluation of which metrics are better/worse for measuring government quality
- Academic and non-academic draft documents

**Not yet developed:** Concrete products or tools

### Q2: Does the organization have an overview of available audits/data points?

**Key Finding:** **Not yet** — this is part of the current workstream.

- Our task focuses on qualitative elements to be transformed into quantitative solutions
- This overview is on the to-do list
- Must be coupled with conceptual work to develop an interdependent list of variables

### Q3: Has research been conducted on Belgian and Swedish approaches?

**Key Finding:** Yes, but there is **no direct "recipe" to copy**.

**Swedish Approach:**
- Primarily uses expert surveys
- Employs Bayesian modeling for estimates
- Methodological choices made for international comparability

**Challenge:** These approaches involve methodological choices (like surveys) that may not be optimal for the Dutch context.

### Q4: What is currently the biggest challenge?

**Key Finding:** **Conceptualization** is the critical foundation.

- Conceptualization is the current priority
- It forms the foundation for all subsequent work
- Requires research and iterative refinement to crystallize
- Different report types exist (including incidental research on specific topics)

2. **Digital product/website** easily integrated into existing workflows

- Can we find comparable, consistent elements across institutions that can be extracted from reports?

### Scalability Considerations

- Solution should be scalable **within the Netherlands**
- International scalability is **outside current scope** due to legislative and regulatory complexity
