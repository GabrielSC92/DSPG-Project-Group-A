# DSPG-Project-Group-A
Quality of Dutch Government - Project

Context:
The Institute of the Quality of the Dutch Government (working title) aims to build a high-
quality longitudinal database of the quality and performance of the Dutch government. For
that purpose, it seeks to compile a wide range of sources of data, and translate them into
easy-to-comprehend, reliable indicators of how well the government is performing on a
series of important process and outcome aspects. There is a wealth of available data which
could inform the database, collected regularly by various governmental and non-
governmental organizations. Some of this data is in ready-to-use format: quantitative and
longitudinal. However, there is also plenty of regularly collected potentially highly
informative data that is presented in reports, usually taking a qualitative format.

Main question:
How can qualitative reports on the performance of the government be reliably translated
into quantitative indicators using LLMs?

Sub-questions:
• What kind of indicators pertaining the quality and performance of the government
    (and its various organizations) could be extracted from the regularly published
    reports by the Dutch Court of Audit (external auditor) the Auditdienst Rijk (internal
    auditor) and/or Directie Internationaal Onderzoek en Beleidsevaluatie (IOB)?
• What considerations must be taken into account in order to ensure the validity and
    reliability of the extracted quantitative indicators?
• What are the opportunities to build a prototype to scale the extraction of data from
    the provided organizations, so that the extraction of data could be done
    continuously, and potentially using other qualitative data sources?

Objective: 
The aim of this research project is to explore the opportunities for translating
qualitative data on the quality of the Dutch government into quantitative indicators. The
goal is to develop a tool which would allow for the continuous extraction of data from
regularly published reports.

Elaboration of the project:
• Analysis of the possibilities: Familiarize yourselves with the Dutch Court of Audit,
    Auditdienst Rijk and/or IOB reports: what kinds of reports do they publish, about
    what government organizations, and what kind of information do they contain? This
    will provide the grounds for identifying what kinds of indicators would be possible to
    extract from the reports. The goal is to have systematic and longitudinal data
    collection process.
• Reliability and validity considerations: The extracted quantitative data would be
    useful only if its quality is high. Here it is important to consider key measurement
    issues: how can the validity and reliability of the extracted data be maximized?
• Build a tech demo: Build a tool that relies on an LLM, which could consistently
    extract quantitative data from qualitative reports, that builds on the analysis
    performed in the previous two steps.
• Critical reflection on risks and opportunities: Analyze the performance of the tool in
    terms of the quality of the data it produces. Offer a critical reflection on the
    possibilities provided by the tool, but also its limitations.

Deliverables:
• A tech demo of a natural language system (e.g. LLM) which could be used to
    extract quantitative indicators from published governmental and non-
    governmental reports.
• A report containing:
    o Critical discussion on the opportunities and obstacles for using LLMs
        for the outlined task.
    o Discussion on how the validity and reliability of the extracted data
        could be ensured using LLMs as an extraction tool.
    o Recommendations on when such tool could be best employed for the
        given task, and when the risks outweigh the benefits.
